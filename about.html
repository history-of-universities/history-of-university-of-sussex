About the project

An independent history

This documents a book writing project by Prof. David M. Berry on the history of the University of Sussex, the first of the plate-glass universities of the 1960s. It assembles, in one place, the institution's founding, its architecture, its people, and its development from the 1961 Royal Charter to the present.

The site is part of the History of Universities project. It is not an official publication of the University of Sussex, and any errors are the project's own.

Method & sources

How it is written

The account draws on published institutional histories, archival material, and the documentary record. References are gathered on the Sources page and cited in the text as the chapters are written. Where a claim is not yet sourced, it is marked as provisional.

The site is built as plain HTML and CSS with no framework and no build step, and is served as a static site. Its branding uses the founding heraldic colours of 1961–62; see The Arms.

Status

Early days. The structure is in place and the chapters are being written. The home page and the heraldry page are drafted; the founding, campus, timeline, and people sections are in preparation.
